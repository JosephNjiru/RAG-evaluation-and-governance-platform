# Human review protocol

## When review is required

Human review is required for high-risk questions, ambiguous questions, unsupported questions, adversarial questions, failed evaluation records and any record with a safety flag.

## Review steps

1. Read the question, answer, retrieved chunks and citations.
2. Check whether each answer claim is supported by cited evidence.
3. Check whether the answer should have refused.
4. Check whether human review was correctly flagged.
5. Record the decision, reviewer notes and any needed correction.

## High-risk outputs

High-risk outputs should not be accepted on automated scores alone. A reviewer must confirm evidence support, citation accuracy and policy fit.

## Unsupported answers

Unsupported answers should be refused, revised or routed for corpus improvement. A plausible answer without evidence is not acceptable.

## Reviewer disagreements

Reviewer disagreements should record the question ID, original decision, revised decision, evidence sections considered and reason for the disagreement.
